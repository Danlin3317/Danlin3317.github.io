My Mental Practice Guide

Chapter 2 Action Observation

What is Action Observation?

A simple type of mental practice is action observation. This is when you observe the movements of other people. For example, you watch a person who is sat opposite from you at a table. You see them lifting a cup from the table, moving it toward their mouth and taking a drink, before they replace the cup back down on the table. This type of mental practice can be an effective method for copying and re-learning movements following a stroke. See link to evidence:

How do I do action observation?

You will deliberately observer the action when you watch an activity video. For example, you want to observer the activity ‘drinking from a cup’. First, you set in a comfortable armchair. A ‘drinking from a cup’ video display is placed 90 cm away from you at the eye level. Try to learn and remember every key movement in the video, e.g. how does the person lift his hand and straighten his arm? How do his fingers reach the cup?
