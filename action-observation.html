Chapter 3 Action Observation

3.1 What is Action Observation?

Another mental practice technique is action observation has also been used as an effective rehabilitation method for stroke survivors. Action observation refers to the deliberate and structured observation of human movement. For example, you watch a person is moving from the bed to a chair in a video. You observe and learn his every movement.

3.2 How to do action observation?

You will deliberately observer the action when you watch an activity video. For example, you want to observer the activity ‘drinking from a cup’. First, you set in a comfortable armchair. A ‘drinking from a cup’ video display is placed 90 cm away from you at the eye level. Try to learn and remember every key movement in the video, e.g. how does the person lift his hand and straighten his arm? How do his fingers reach the cup?
