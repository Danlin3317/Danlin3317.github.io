My Mental Practice Guide

Combined motor imagery and action observation

What is combined action observation and motor imagery (AO+MI)?

Previously, you learned what action observation (AO) and motor imagery (MI) are as two separate tools for stroke rehabilitation. Here we introduce you to the idea that these two methods can also be done at the same time (simultaneously). When using these two methods separately, you first observe a movement demonstration, and then after the video has ended you imagine doing the movement yourself. You can also alternate between seeing and imagining. The combined AO+MI approach is different. This is when you observe the video demonstration and at the same time you imagine the feeling and effort of performing the action shown in the video. You must use the video as a visual guide for what you should be imagining in real-time.

How do I do AO+MI?

You will imagine yourself doing an activity. At the very same time (simultaneously) you will view the same action displayed on the screen. For example, you are imagining yourself reaching to a cup on a table. Imagine the feeling and effort as you extend your arm and open your hand to reach and grasp the cup. While you are doing this, you will be watching a video recorded from a first person perspective â€“ the video shows a hand reaching and grasping the cup. Try to synchronise your imagined action with the action shown in the display. You can use the video as a helpful guide for your own imagined action.
