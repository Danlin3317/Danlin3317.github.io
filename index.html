Chapter 1 Mental Practice Technique

1.1 What is Mental Practice Technique?

Mental practice technique involves activation of the neural system while a person imagines performing a task or body movement without actual physically performing the movement. In other words, you mentally visualize yourself moving your paralyzed or weak muscles can be another treatment option. Mental practice technique includes motor imagery and action observation.

1.2 How might it help?

Studies report increased blood flow, electromyographic activity, and resulting changes in the motor cortex in stroke patients after mental practice. In other words, major changes are happening in the brain when you visualize yourself moving.
